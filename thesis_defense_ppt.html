Problem Statement

Research Gap

  • Secondary Philippine cities like Dagupan remain systematically underexplored in UHI literature
  • No peer-reviewed studies have specifically examined Dagupan's thermal environment
  • Existing adaptation efforts lack spatially explicit evidence for targeting interventions

Why Dagupan City?

Geographic Vulnerability

  • Agno River delta location increases solar radiation exposure
  • Reduced cloud cover vs. neighboring areas
  • Extensive wetland loss reduces natural cooling

Socioeconomic Exposure

  • "Bangus Capital" - aquaculture vulnerable to heat stress
  • Outdoor labor (vendors, drivers, enforcers) at risk
  • Heat-related fish kills threaten livelihoods

Research Objectives

1 LST Mapping

Generate Land Surface Temperature (LST) maps of Dagupan City for the dry seasons (April-May) of 2016, 2019, 2022, and 2025 using cloud-free Landsat 8/9 satellite imagery

2 Biophysical Relationships

Assess spatial relationships between LST and three biophysical indices through Pearson correlation analysis:

  • NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index)
  • NDBI (Normalized Difference Built-up Index)
  • NDWI (Normalized Difference Water Index)

3 Heat-Vulnerable Barangays

Identify heat-vulnerable barangays by detecting statistically significant LST hotspot clusters using Local Moran's I spatial autocorrelation in GeoDa software
